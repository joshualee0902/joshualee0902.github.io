About Us

Meet the Johns Creek Sports Center leadership team.

Joshua Lee: Co-President

Class of 2022
Main sport(s): Soccer, Basketball
Email: joshualee0902@gmail.com

As I have observed in my middle school years and first 2 years in high school, the athletic community and the rest of the student body seemed very separated. As an advent sports fan and leisure player, I felt that I could use my enthusiasm towards sports in a way that could help the Johns Creek High School community. ESPN was an inspiration to this club, which I thought we can carry out various activities that are entertaining to both the athletic and non-athletic part of the student body. My fellow peers and I aim in raising common knowledge of our school’s athletic activities, which could bring together J ohns Creek High School as a whole, also possibly bringing better results to Johns Creek Athletics.

Yifan Wang: Co-President

Class of 2022
Main sport(s): Basketball, Cross Country
Email: yifanwang.rt@gmail.com

I was in the school Cross Country team since freshman year and played basketball throughout my life. I also enjoy watching sports during my free time. As a student of JCHS, I can feel the students want to connect with the school athletes, so we decided to develop a platform that students can connect with the athletes easier than ever by providing stories, live streams, and highlights about all the sports in our school.

Kevin Park: Co-President

Class of 2022
Main sport(s): Tennis
Email: kevinpark237@gmail.com

Sports have always been part of my life. I played soccer when I was very young then baseball in elementary to now tennis. I love both watching and playing sports at any time of the year. But, I think the most important part about sports is the community and the unity that it forms. Teams create bonds with one another through sports and even fans develop new friendships through sports events. Therefore, my colleagues and I plan to create a closer relationship between the student body and school sports. Johns Creek High School has a lot of thriving sports fans but could still use more school spirit by bringing in more people. I really want people to feel passionate about sports and take interest in it as much as I do.

Matthew Zimmerman: Fall/Winter Sports Manager

Class of 2021
Main sport(s): Football, Basketball, Baseball, Soccer
Email: matthewzimm5@gmail.com

Growing up in a sports-loving family, I have always had a unique passion for sports as a kid. Pretty much every sport you can think of my family has played before. I swam and was involved in Tae Kwon Do as a kid and young teen, my brother played soccer and currently runs track, and my sister is on a college golf team. Whether it's in the standings or on a couch, I love watching a competitive sports game for fun. Even though this is my last year at Johns Creek, I can’t wait to connect the athletic and non-athletic community through this amazing platform my fellow friends have created and look forward to making a truly positive impact in our community with the sports we all love.

Yongha Cho: Spring Sports Manager

Class of 2022
Main sport(s): Soccer
Email: Yongha2162@gmail.com

I have always enjoyed playing sports. Since I was young, I played a variety of sports with my friends. Not only me, but also my friends and students in Johns Creek High School watch sports enthusiastically. Our school students often go to the stadium to support our team. Also, after the game, we often discuss the previous game. However, I usually felt that our school lacks a school community. Also, the news and the highlights of the games are hard to access. This thought led me and my friends to establish Johns Creek Sport Center. My main goal for this club is to bring the sport community closer and have more active communication with the friends about school sports. Moreover, I want to attract students who don’t watch sports to have more interest in school sports.

Kyle Kim: E-Sports Manager

Class of 2022
Main e-sport(s): League of Legends
Email: kylekim1234567891011@gmail.com

E-sports is a big passion of mine and especially from League of Legends. Not to mention it’s rapidly growing in popularity from around the world. I’ve never really been active but e-sports was a different way to have a relationship with friends other than going outside and playing a typical sport. This can also be a conduit to get other people more into team activities seeing as there are many introverted people who love playing video games. My goal for this club is to recognize e sports as a viable option for team building and not just typical sports. This can then build a new appreciation for others trying to improve in the realm of E sports.

About JCSC

Johns Creek Sports Center is a club that aims in adjoining the athletic and non-athletic community. We plan on carrying out such a goal by providing entertainment with articles, highlight videos, podcasts, and in-school activities that would gather more attention to Johns Creek athletic events.
